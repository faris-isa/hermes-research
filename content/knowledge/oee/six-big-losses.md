# The Six Big Losses

The Six Big Losses framework categorizes all production losses into three groups that map directly to the [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness|OEE formula]].

## Overview

| # | Loss | Category | OEE Factor |
|---|------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Equipment Failure | Unplanned Stop | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Availability]] |
| 2 | Setup & Adjustment | Planned Stop | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Availability]] |
| 3 | Idling & Minor Stops | Speed Loss | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Performance]] |
| 4 | Reduced Speed | Speed Loss | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Performance]] |
| 5 | Process Defects | Quality Loss | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Quality]] |
| 6 | Reduced Yield | Quality Loss | [[OEE — Overall Equipment Effectiveness#The Formula\|Quality]] |

---

## Availability Losses
*Periods when equipment is stopped*

### 1. Equipment Failure (Unplanned Stop)

Significant downtime due to failure or unplanned event.

**Examples:**
- Tooling failure, breakdowns
- Unplanned maintenance
- Lack of operators or materials
- Starved/blocked by upstream/downstream

> **Threshold:** Downtime > 2 min = Equipment Failure; ≤ 2 min = Minor Stop

### 2. Setup & Adjustment (Planned Stop)

Downtime due to changeover or adjustment.

**Examples:**
- Changeovers (make ready)
- Major adjustments
- Cleaning, warmup
- Planned maintenance

**Key methodology:** [[OEE Improvement Strategies#SMED (Single-Minute Exchange of Die)|SMED]] is the primary approach for reducing Setup & Adjustment losses.

---

## Performance Losses
*Equipment running below optimal speed*

### 3. Idling & Minor Stops

Short stops (1–2 min) resolved by operator without maintenance.

**Examples:**
- Misfeeds, material jams
- Obstructed flow
- Incorrect settings
- Misaligned sensors
- Periodic quick cleaning

> **Key insight:** Minor stops are chronic and often overlooked but accumulate significantly. Most companies don't track them accurately.

### 4. Reduced Speed

Running slower than Ideal Cycle Time.

**Examples:**
- Dirty/worn equipment
- Poor lubrication
- Substandard materials
- Operator inexperience
- Startup/shutdown periods

---

## Quality Losses
*Parts that don't meet specifications*

### 5. Process Defects

Defective parts during **stable (steady-state) production** — includes scrap AND rework.

> **OEE uses First Pass Yield** — reworked parts count as quality loss.

**Examples:**
- Incorrect settings
- Operator errors
- Lot expiration

### 6. Reduced Yield

Defective parts from **startup until stable production** — most common after changeovers.

**Examples:**
- Suboptimal changeovers
- Incorrect settings
- Equipment warmup cycles
- Inherent startup waste
