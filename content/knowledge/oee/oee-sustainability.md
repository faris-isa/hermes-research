# OEE and Sustainability

## The OEE-Energy Connection

Improving OEE directly reduces energy consumption and carbon footprint:

1. **Higher Availability** = Less idle time = Less energy wasted on standby
2. **Higher Performance** = Fewer micro-stops = Less energy per unit produced
3. **Higher Quality** = Less scrap/rework = Less energy wasted on defective products

## Quantified Impact

- **10% OEE improvement** typically yields **5-8% energy reduction**
- **Reduced downtime** means fewer startup/shutdown cycles (energy-intensive)
- **Fewer defects** means less material waste and reprocessing energy

## Carbon Footprint Reduction

- Industrial sector accounts for **~23% of total U.S. greenhouse gas emissions**
- Energy efficiency can reduce industrial carbon emissions by **up to 34%** in many sectors
- OEE improvement is a practical pathway to both operational excellence AND sustainability goals

## The Hidden Factory and Energy Waste

The **Hidden Factory** represents not just lost production capacity, but also wasted energy:

```
Hidden Factory Energy = Total Energy Consumed - Energy Used for Good Production
```

For many manufacturers, the **energy wasted in the hidden factory exceeds the energy used for actual production**.

## Sustainability-OEE Alignment

| Sustainability Goal | OEE Contribution |
|---------------------|------------------|
| Reduce energy consumption | Higher OEE = less energy per good unit |
| Minimize waste | Higher quality = less scrap/rework |
| Optimize resource use | Higher availability = better asset utilization |
| Reduce carbon footprint | All three factors combined |
| Meet ESG targets | Quantifiable operational metrics |
